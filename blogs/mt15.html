Yamaha MT-15 BS 6 Review – Beast is in the House

Hey Everyone,
Welcome to a brand new blog of our series Motopsychcle Throttle, In this one we Review a superb machine from the Yamaha stable called the MT-15.
The motorcycle is in front of us and it’s time for us to review.
Let’s talk about the design first;
At first glance, the bike looks modern and sleek in terms of appearance,
The Bike has MT Series DNA built in, which makes it a miniature version of the Hyper naked bigger sibling.
The design philosophy seems to be focused on compactness and aggression working in tandem starting right From the front, where the Bi functional LED headlight gives it a aggressive look and feel for which the MT series is known for. The down chin face design gives it a beast like stance.
The spherical shaped tank looks curvy and aerodynamic, it is covered by a well designed cover to make it look completely different from the rest of the pack. The upright forks in the front look strong and sturdy.
Other design elements such as the short rising tail, front winglets, radiator side fins and compact exhaust seem to fit the next gen design scheme perfectly.
The combination of black with grey and orange alloys seems to be a bold but interesting choice of colours that seems to gel with overall design scheme of the bike. The colour scheme is bright and vibrant, it evokes a sense of energy, aggression and excitement among the onlookers.
MT-15’s soft wide seat offers comfort while its easy-to-hold grab-bar looks great and offers better grip to the pillion rider.
The New MT15 design philosophy is sharp and edgy which makes it a fantastic looker from all angles. The design feels fresh and modern, in terms of design the bike will appeal to most potential buyers.
Overall, In terms of Design, We give it: 4.45/5.
Performance

The Yamaha MT-15 comes with a 155cc LC 4V Liquid Cooled Engine which is now BS-VI compliant, the engine generates a power output of 18.6PS@10000rpm and a Torque figure of 13.9Nm@8500rpm.
The VVA system installed in the bike provides punchy low end torque and ensures consistent power and torque delivery throughout the entire rev range. The engine feels smooth while the initial power delivery is instant due to light weight of the bike, As we go up in the rev range, the power delivery of the bike feels linear and consistent. The VVA System provides punchy performance especially in the low rev range of the bike.
The disc brakes in the bike work well and due to assist and slip clutch, the gearshifts feel smooth and responsive.
In terms of ride and comfort
The seating position is nice and comfortable, switches are located in convenient positions, and the ergonomics seem to be on a comfortable scale.
The 140/70R17M/C 66H radial tyre fitted at the rear and a 100/80-17M/C 52P – Tubeless Tyre in the front combine to provide excellent grip, the bike feels light and nimble when you ride, the handling seems to be on point due to a fantastic Deltabox frame.
The excellent suspension settings of the bike help gobble up bad roads in an instant.
Overall the motorcycle feels quick of the ground with excellent low end torque and linear power delivery, if you’re looking for slick, compact, modern streetbike, you can look into the MT-15, it might not be the ultimate motorcycle in terms of performance, but it checks most boxes you expect from modern entry level streetbike, it is great for riding in crowded city streets and to go on occasional highway rides.
If an entry level streetbike is what you’re looking for with design as your main preference, then MT-15 is a great option as it combines excellent design with good performance and great handling capabilities, go and test ride the MT-15.
In terms of performance, we give it: 3.98/5.
